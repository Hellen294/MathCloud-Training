Introduction

This unit is designed to help educators prepare for integrating technology into their classrooms using Google tools. The focus is on setting up the necessary infrastructure, familiarizing with basic functionalities, and initiating the first steps towards a tech-enhanced learning environment.

Basic Setup

Creating a Google Account

Steps:

Visit accounts.google.com.

Click on "Create account".

Follow the prompts to create a new Google account using your school email if available.

Purpose: A Google account is essential for accessing Google Classroom and other G Suite for Education tools.

Setting Up Google Classroom

Steps:

Go to classroom.google.com.

Sign in with your Google account.

Click on the "+" sign and select "Create class".

Fill in class details (name, section, subject, room).

Purpose: This sets up the virtual space where students can join, receive assignments, and participate in class activities.

Installing Necessary Apps

Steps:

Install Google Classroom, Google Drive, Google Docs, Google Slides, and Google Forms from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store.

Purpose: Ensures all necessary tools are available for both the teacher and students to interact seamlessly.

Initial Classroom Integration

Creating and Sharing Your First Assignment

Steps:

Open Google Classroom.

Go to the "Classwork" tab and click "Create".

Choose "Assignment", fill in the details (title, instructions, due date).

Attach relevant files from Google Drive.

Click "Assign" to share with students.

Purpose: Familiarizes both teacher and students with the process of assignment creation and submission.

Organizing Class Materials

Steps:

Use Google Drive to create folders for different subjects or units.

Share folders with students or colleagues.

Upload important resources (syllabus, reading materials, multimedia).

Purpose: Keeps all class materials organized and easily accessible.

Using Google Meet for Virtual Classes

Steps:

Schedule a meeting in Google Calendar.

Add Google Meet video conferencing details.

Share the meeting link with students via Google Classroom.

Conduct your virtual class, utilizing screen sharing and interactive tools.

Purpose: Facilitates remote teaching and virtual face-to-face interaction.

Engaging Students with Google Forms

Steps:

Go to Google Forms and create a new form.

Design quizzes or feedback forms using various question types (multiple choice, short answer).

Share the form link via Google Classroom.

Collect and review student responses.

Purpose: Engages students in interactive activities and assessments, and collects valuable feedback.

Tips for Success

Consistency: Use Google Classroom as your central hub for all class activities to maintain organization and clarity for students.

Practice: Spend time exploring each tool and its features to become comfortable and efficient in their use.
