Introduction

This unit focuses on using Google tools to streamline and organize activities, both for personal efficiency and collaborative tasks. Effective organization is key to managing a productive classroom and ensuring that students and colleagues are aligned and engaged.

Using Google Calendar

Setting Up Your Calendar

Steps:

Go to Google Calendar.

Sign in with your Google account.

Click on the “Create” button to set up new events.

Purpose: Helps in scheduling and managing class activities, meetings, and personal tasks.

Creating and Sharing Events

Steps:

Click on a date and time to create an event.

Add event details such as title, location, and description.

Invite guests by adding their email addresses.

Click "Save" to add the event to your calendar and send invitations.

Purpose: Ensures all participants are aware of upcoming events and can plan accordingly.

Setting Reminders and Notifications

Steps:

Open an event and click on the “Add notification” option.

Choose how and when you want to receive reminders (e.g., email, pop-up).

Purpose: Keeps you and your students on track with timely reminders for important tasks and deadlines.

Organizing Class Activities

Using Google Tasks

Steps:

Access Google Tasks through the sidebar in Gmail or Google Calendar.

Create task lists for different subjects or projects.

Add tasks with details and due dates.

Purpose: Breaks down projects into manageable steps, ensuring nothing is overlooked.

Creating To-Do Lists

Steps:

Use Google Keep or Google Docs to create and share to-do lists.

Organize tasks by priority and due date.

Share lists with colleagues or students for collaborative projects.

Purpose: Keeps track of tasks and responsibilities, promoting accountability.

Collaborating with Google Drive

Setting Up Shared Folders

Steps:

Go to Google Drive.

Create a new folder and name it appropriately (e.g., “Math Assignments”).

Share the folder with students or colleagues by clicking the “Share” button and entering their email addresses.

Purpose: Centralizes resources and assignments, making them easily accessible to everyone involved.

Collaborating on Documents

Steps:

Open Google Docs, Sheets, or Slides.

Create a new document or open an existing one.

Share the document with collaborators.

Use comments and suggestions to provide feedback and make edits.

Purpose: Facilitates real-time collaboration and ensures that everyone is on the same page.

Managing Communication with Gmail and Google Chat

Organizing Your Inbox

Steps:

Use labels to categorize emails.

Set up filters to automatically sort incoming messages.

Archive or delete emails to keep your inbox uncluttered.

Purpose: Maintains an organized and manageable inbox, ensuring important messages are not missed.

Using Google Chat for Instant Communication

Steps:

Access Google Chat from Gmail or directly at Google Chat.

Create chat groups for different classes or projects.

Use direct messages for quick, informal communication.

Purpose: Enhances communication efficiency and helps in quickly addressing questions or issues.

Tips for Success

Consistency: Regularly update your calendar and task lists to reflect new events and tasks.

Integration: Use Google Calendar and Tasks in conjunction with Gmail to keep all your tools interconnected.

Collaboration: Encourage students and colleagues to actively use and update shared documents and folders.
